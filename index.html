What is Momo?

Momo is a type of dumpling native to Nepal and Tibet. It consists of a thin wheat-flour wrapper filled with various ingredients, including meat or vegetables. The dumplings are steamed, fried, or boiled.

History of Momo

"Momo was originally a delicacy among the Newar community in Kathmandu and later spread across Nepal and beyond." — Nepali Food Culture

Types of Momo

  • Steamed Momo
  • Fried Momo
  • C-Momo (Chili Momo)
  • Jhol Momo (Soup Momo)
  • Kothey Momo (Pan-fried Momo)

How to Make Momo

Below is a simple recipe for making delicious steamed momo at home.

  1. Prepare the dough with flour, water, and salt.
  2. Make the filling using minced meat or vegetables, onions, garlic, and spices.
  3. Wrap the filling in thin momo wrappers.
  4. Steam for 10-12 minutes until fully cooked.
